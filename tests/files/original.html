Opening Ceremony Safety and Security
As we prepare for the day-long celebrations of the Diamond Jubilee Opening Ceremony, we would like to advise the Jamat to take appropriate safety measures to protect homes, businesses, and vehicles while away for the event. Please ensure that your home and business alarms are correctly armed, and park your vehicles securely. If you are traveling for the Opening Ceremony and plan to be away for an extended period of time, please remember to manage your mail accordingly. Additionally, Law Enforcement agencies generally advise individuals not to post vacation information on social media until after you have returned. For more information on securing your property, please see the flyer.

Jubilee Children’s Book Drive
The Jamat is invited to participate in the Jubilee Children’s Book Drive and donate new or gently children’s books appropriate for ages 2 and above in the designated area in each Jamatkhana. The books will be donated on behalf of the Ismaili Community to not-for-profit organizations that work with children to enhance education and literacy.

One Jamat Celebration Dance Workshops
Celebrating the auspicious occasion of Diamond Jubilee comes in many forms, including dance. The Jamat is invited to celebrate the ethic of pluralism on Imamat Day by participating in the One Jamat Celebration Dance. The music and steps in this specially composed and choreographed dance reflects the diversity of the USA Jamat. Workshops will be held in each Jamatkhana for the Jamat to learn the new communal dance, so that we can come together on Imamat Day to celebrate as One Jamat. Please see representatives at your respective Jamatkhana for more information.

We are pleased that the Jamat has been participating in the workshops in large numbers. The instructional video has also been made available at TheIsmailiUSA.org for the Jamat to learn and practice the dance outside of the workshops.

Time and Knowledge Nazrana (TKN)
TKN is a murid’s gift to the Imam of the Time on the occasion of Diamond Jubilee, and TKN Niyats submitted have no time limit for completion. Jamati members who submitted a TKN Niyat during the Golden Jubilee, whether or not they have been called upon to serve, may still submit a new Niyat for the Diamond Jubilee. Procedure to Register:
  • TKN offerings are currently ongoing. Jamati members wishing to offer their Niyat for the Diamond Jubilee TKN are requested to do so at the designated area in Jamatkhana.
  • Jamati members who have already obtained a TKN registration card are requested to complete the process of creating their profile online by visiting the TKN website at www.timeandknowledge.org
TKN Niyats can be offered until July 2018. For more information, please contact TKN volunteers in Jamatkhana or visit the TKN website.
